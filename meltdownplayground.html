MELTDOWN PLAYGROUND

Figma Collaboration with Lindsay Mehl.

The Problem

୨୧ · · ♡ · · ୨୧

  • Dining and ordering experiences have become lengthy and challenging for users. Customers typically get confused by long lines for cashier ordering, complicated menu interactions, and difficulty navigating through the space to find seating.

Our Mission

୨୧ · · ♡ · · ୨୧

  • Taking inspiration from Frita Batidos’ use of the casual dining experience seen through communal tables and counter-service ordering, we want to solve issues that were present in the already existing design for casual dining. We hope that by creating a digital ordering system that helps users understand what they're ordering, we may reduce anxiety, confusion, and long wait times that occur when ordering from a register operator. We aim to include descriptions of menu items, opportunities to note allergies, and favorites/specials on our monitor to optimize the user experience. Our goal is to make a dining experience that invites university students to find community and feel comfortable hanging out in our space without pressures that are present in current designs.

Emphasis: making the dining process more accessible and easy for all groups

୨୧ · · ♡ · · ୨୧

  • 1. The product should easily allow users who are aware of digital ordering to order on a digital ordering system in the restaurant, reducing wait times and anxiety associated with counter-service. (Ease of use)
  • 2. The product should communicate information on the menu in a way that is easy to learn and comprehend in a timely manner. The design will be accessible for various users to read. (Learnability)
  • 3. The product should allow users to feel comfortable finding seating in the restaurant and avoid overcrowding. (Comfortability)
